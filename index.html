Why True Offset?

With True Offset, you can trust that each donation will prevent one ton of carbon from being released into the atmosphere through the design, build, and operation of solar and battery infrastructure to displace existing fossil fuel electricity producers.

How we build trust→

High Quality Offsets. High Project Visibility.

Currently, carbon offset market is unregulated, resulting in many low quality, opaque carbon offsets that can do more harm than good. With the carbon offset market expected to grow exponentially over the next decade, it is essential that High Quality Carbon Offsets are created. Here's how we create them.

Non-Profit

This organization is not constrained by a profit hungry board and all profits must be reinvested in the business. If bankrupcy or dissolution occurs, all assets must be donated to an existing Non-Profit or local government utility.

Powered by Systems Engineering Fundamentals

By using the project milestones and reporting framework created by NASA, you can view project deliverables and attend donor meetings relating to your funded projects.

Additional

Each True Offset Project creates new solar and battery infrastructure that is financed via your contributions; these projects cannot exist without your donations.

Not Overestimated

Each project's offset is calculated by dividing the total cost of the project by the expected lifetime output and the present and predicted future conditions of the local grid. Once built, tracking will occur to help build future models and validate offset integrity to forecasts.

Permanent

Each True Offset Project will be planned from conception to decommissioning and replacement of the old infrastructure.

Not Claimed by Another Entity

Each True Offset carbon credit is certified to be yours only. Double issueance, use, and claiming are strictly prohibited.

Not associated with Significant Social or Environmental Harms

All infrastructure providors and contractors must be vetted to ensure no assosciation with signifiant social or environmental harms.
